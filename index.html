Behind the screen.

Hi, I'm Anna, a UX/UI designer with a background that might surprise you. Before diving into design, I studied social work, which taught me a lot about how people think, what they need, and why they behave the way they do. Turns out, that translates pretty well to screens.

For the past two years I've been studying digital design at Broby Grafiska, where I've been shaping that understanding into a design practice. Along the way I've also picked up the basics of HTML and CSS, enough to know how things are built and to work closely with developers. I'm drawn to the psychology behind how people navigate and experience digital spaces, what makes something feel easy and natural, and what makes it feel confusing. I use AI as a creative and technical collaborator, helping me build, iterate, and problem solve faster so I can focus on the design thinking behind it.

This portfolio is a collection of work from my time in school, a reflection of where I am now and where I'm headed.

Mineral Kingdom

Year2025
TypeBrand Identity (Fictive)
RoleUX/UI Designer
ToolsFigma, Illustrator
A branding and UX project exploring how Filipstad can make the Mineral Kingdom a more attractive destination for geotourism and nature tourism.

Filipstad has something rare, a landscape shaped by millions of years of geology, rich history, and untouched nature. The Mineral Kingdom sits at the heart of it, but its potential as a destination hasn't fully been realized yet.

The challenge

The challenge was to explore how the Mineral Kingdom could become more than a site to visit, more like a vast, wall-less museum where geology, history, and nature come together into one coherent experience.

This meant looking at the brand, the visual identity, and the digital presence, and asking: how do we make this place feel as interesting as it actually is?

The solution

To make the Mineral Kingdom feel alive and worth visiting, we focused on two things: getting people there, and giving them something to do once they arrived. For the physical experience, we designed a mobile game where visitors scan QR codes placed throughout the park to hunt for hidden minerals. It turns a walk in nature into something interactive and memorable, accessible for families and curious first-timers, without feeling childish for those who take geology seriously. For the website, we built a structure that works for two very different audiences. Casual visitors can quickly get a feel for the place and find a reason to go. And for the geology enthusiasts, the information and depth is there, just not in the way. Together, the two parts push toward the same goal: making the Mineral Kingdom a place that's easy to discover, and hard to forget.

Glam & Groove

Year2025
TypeBrand Identity
RoleUX/UI Designer
ToolsFigma, Illustrator, Premiere Pro
Where dance meets community.

Glam and Groove is a Karlstad-based dance organization bringing people together through urban dance styles, creating an inclusive and accessible space where young people can grow, express themselves, and find community.

The Challenge

Glam and Groove has spent a decade building a passionate, community-driven dance movement in Karlstad, but their external communication and visual identity don't yet reflect the energy, warmth, and impact they bring. How might we help Glam and Groove show up in the world the way they show up for their community, ensuring their message reaches and resonates with more young people across the Värmland region?

The Solution

To ensure Glam and Groove shows up in the world the way they show up for their community, we will craft a complete visual identity, including a new logo, colors, typography, imagery, and tone of voice, paired with a campaign film and a fully responsive website. Together, these deliverables will give their message the consistency, strength, and visibility it deserves, helping them connect with more young people and making it instantly clear to anyone who encounters them what Glam and Groove truly stands for.

Optimize the Menu Finder

Year2025
TypeOptimizing website
RoleUX/UI Designer
Tools Figma, Google Lighthouse, Hotjar
The Menu Finder is a website where you can discover restaurants near you, browse their menus, find what you're in the mood for, and decide where to eat.

The Challenge

The main goal was to make the Menu Finder the go-to place when people are looking for a restaurant. To get there, the focus was on creating an experience smooth enough to keep users on the page, and good enough to make them want to come back.

The Solution

I started by mapping out where things went wrong and worked through it step by step. Load times were cut down so users wouldn't drop off before getting started, and all broken links were fixed so people could actually move through the app without getting stuck. The navigation was rebuilt to make finding a menu simple and straightforward. And instead of hiding the calls to action, I made them clear and easy to spot, placed right where users needed them. The result is an experience that's easier to use, easier to stay on, and worth coming back to.
